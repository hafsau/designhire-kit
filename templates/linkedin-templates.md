# LinkedIn Message Templates

Short, personal messages that don't sound like everyone else's.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Keep it short** — 3-5 sentences max
2. **Be specific** — reference their work or the company's
3. **Don't beg** — soft ask, not hard sell
4. **Sound human** — no corporate speak

---

## Template 1: After Applying (Hiring Manager)

```
Hi [Name] — I just applied for the [Role] at [Company]. I noticed [specific thing about their work or company's recent design]. [One sentence connecting to your experience]. [Soft ask or conversation starter].
```

**Example:**
> Hi Sarah — I just applied for the Design Engineer role at Figma. I noticed you led the new variable fonts feature — the preview panel complexity is similar to a challenge I tackled in healthcare (progressive disclosure for medication options). Would love to hear how your team approached the technical constraints.

---

## Template 2: After Applying (Recruiter)

```
Hi [Name] — I just applied for the [Role]. My background is in [relevant experience], and I've [specific accomplishment]. [What drew you to this role]. Happy to chat if the fit looks good.
```

**Example:**
> Hi Jamie — I just applied for the Senior Designer role at Stripe. My background is in complex B2B tools (fintech and healthcare), and I've shipped design systems at scale. The Dashboard work Stripe's team has done is exactly what I want to be building. Happy to chat if the fit looks good.

---

## Template 3: Networking (Before Applying)

```
Hi [Name] — I've been following [Company]'s design work, especially [specific project or blog post]. I'm exploring [type of role] opportunities and curious how [specific question]. No pressure to respond — just wanted to reach out.
```

**Example:**
> Hi Marcus — I've been following Linear's design work, especially your recent blog post on building the mobile app. I'm exploring design engineering roles and curious how your team balances speed vs. craft in sprints. No pressure to respond — just wanted to reach out.

---

## Template 4: Referral Request (Someone You Know)

```
Hi [Name] — I saw [Company] has a [Role] open. I know you [work there / know someone there]. Would you be comfortable making an intro, or letting me know if I should apply cold? Totally understand if not — just thought I'd ask.
```

**Example:**
> Hi Alex — I saw Notion has a Product Designer role open. I know you worked with their design team last year. Would you be comfortable making an intro, or letting me know if I should apply cold? Totally understand if not — just thought I'd ask.

---

## Template 5: Post-Interview Follow-Up (Connection Request)

```
Hi [Name] — thanks for the conversation [today/yesterday]. I enjoyed [specific thing discussed]. Looking forward to next steps. [Optional: offer to share something discussed].
```

**Example:**
> Hi Sarah — thanks for the conversation today. The challenge you described with onboarding flows is interesting — I have some thoughts I didn't get to share. Looking forward to next steps. Happy to sketch out that idea if useful.

---

## What NOT to Write

**Don't:**
- "I hope this message finds you well"
- "I'd love to connect and learn more"
- "I'm passionate about..."
- "I'd love to pick your brain"
- "Your company is amazing"
- Multiple exclamation points
- Generic compliments

**Don't open with:**
- "Dear Hiring Manager"
- "I am reaching out regarding..."
- "I wanted to introduce myself"

---

## Timing Tips

- **After applying:** Wait 1-2 days, then message
- **Before applying:** Message first, mention you're about to apply
- **After interview:** Same day or next morning
- **Follow up if no response:** Wait 1 week, send once more, then stop

---

## Response Rate Reality

Most messages won't get responses. That's normal. Focus on:
- Quality over quantity
- Specific references that show you did research
- Making it easy for them to respond (ask one clear thing)

A 10-20% response rate is good. Don't take silence personally.
