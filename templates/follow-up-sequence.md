# Follow-Up Sequence

A structured plan for following up after applying to a job.

---

## The 7-Day Follow-Up Plan

### Day 0: Apply
- Submit application
- Save job description (they may take it down)
- Note who posted it (hiring manager, recruiter, HR)

### Day 1-2: LinkedIn Outreach
- Find the hiring manager or recruiter on LinkedIn
- Send a short message (use `/linkedin-outreach`)
- Connect request with note if you can't message directly

### Day 3-4: Wait
- Don't send anything else yet
- Check if they viewed your profile

### Day 5-7: Email Follow-Up (Optional)
- Only if you have a direct email
- Keep it shorter than your cover letter
- Reference that you applied + one new piece of info

```
Subject: Following up — [Role] application

Hi [Name],

I applied for the [Role] earlier this week. Since then, I came across [relevant thing — their blog post, news about the company, etc.] and it reinforced why I'm excited about this opportunity.

I'd love to chat about [specific thing]. Let me know if you have questions about my background.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Day 7+: Move On (Mentally)
- Assume no response means no (for now)
- Don't keep following up
- They may come back weeks/months later

---

## After Interview Follow-Up

### Same Day: Thank You Email
- Send within 2-4 hours of interview
- Use `/thank-you` skill
- Reference something specific from the conversation

### Day 2-3: LinkedIn Connect
- Send connection request to interviewers
- Brief note: "Thanks again for the conversation about [topic]"

### If They Gave a Timeline:
- Wait until that date passes
- Then send one follow-up asking for an update

### If No Timeline:
- Wait 5-7 business days
- Send one polite check-in

```
Subject: Checking in — [Role] interview

Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up on our conversation last week about the [Role]. I enjoyed discussing [specific topic] and remain excited about the opportunity.

Is there any additional information I can provide?

Best,
[Your name]
```

---

## How Many Follow-Ups?

**Before interview:** Maximum 2 touches
1. Application
2. LinkedIn message (Day 1-2)

**After interview:** Maximum 3 touches
1. Thank you email (same day)
2. LinkedIn connect (Day 2-3)
3. Check-in if no response (Day 5-7)

After that, wait for them. More follow-ups hurt more than help.

---

## What If They Ghost?

It happens. Common reasons:
- Role filled/frozen/changed
- Too many candidates to respond to everyone
- Internal candidate got it
- Process is slow

**What NOT to do:**
- Send passive-aggressive notes
- Repeatedly message
- Go around them to someone else

**What TO do:**
- Move on
- Leave the door open for future roles
- If you see them at an event, be gracious

## Sample Follow-Up Emails

### After No Response to Application (Day 7)

```
Subject: [Role] at [Company] — quick follow-up

Hi [Name],

I applied for the [Role] last week and wanted to make sure it came through. I'm particularly interested in [specific challenge they're facing] — I tackled something similar at [Company] where I [brief result].

Happy to answer any questions.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### After Interview (No Timeline Given, Day 7)

```
Subject: Following up — [Role]

Hi [Name],

Thanks again for the conversation last [day]. I've been thinking about [specific challenge discussed] and sketched out some initial ideas.

I remain very interested in the role. Any update on timeline or next steps?

Best,
[Your name]
```

### After Missing Their Timeline

```
Subject: Checking in — [Role]

Hi [Name],

Hope you're well. You mentioned you'd be making a decision by [date] — wanted to check if there's any update or additional information I can provide.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

## Tracking Your Applications

Use a simple spreadsheet or Notion database:

| Company | Role | Applied | Contacted | Response | Interview | Status |
|---------|------|---------|-----------|----------|-----------|--------|
| Figma | Designer | 3/1 | 3/2 (LI) | 3/5 | 3/12 | Offer |
| Linear | Design Eng | 3/3 | 3/4 (LI) | — | — | No response |
| Stripe | Senior | 3/5 | 3/6 (email) | 3/8 | — | Phone screen scheduled |

This keeps you from over-following-up or losing track.
