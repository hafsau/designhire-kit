# Cover Letter Structure

Reference this when writing or reviewing cover letters.

---

## The 4-Paragraph Formula

### Paragraph 1: The Hook
- Why THIS role at THIS company
- Not generic excitement — specific interest
- One sentence connecting you to their problem

**Good:**
> "I saw the Senior Product Designer role at Linear and got excited — you're building tools for people who care about speed and clarity, which is exactly what I focused on at [Company]."

**Bad:**
> "I am writing to express my enthusiastic interest in the Product Designer position at your esteemed organization."

---

### Paragraph 2: The Fit
- Your most relevant experience
- Specific project that matches their needs
- Include a metric

**Good:**
> "At MedRec, I redesigned the medication logging workflow that was causing 40% error rates. By implementing progressive disclosure — showing only the 5 most common options instead of 40 — I reduced errors to 5% and improved satisfaction scores from 2.1 to 4.2."

**Bad:**
> "I have extensive experience in UX design and have worked on many successful projects throughout my career."

---

### Paragraph 3: The Connection
- Another relevant skill or project
- OR address something specific from the job description
- Shows breadth without being scattered

**Good:**
> "Your job description mentions design systems — I built and maintained the component library at [Company] across web and mobile, focusing on accessibility. I'm comfortable working with engineers to ship real code, not just designs."

**Bad:**
> "I am also skilled in Figma, Sketch, prototyping, user research, and design systems."

---

### Paragraph 4: The Close
- Brief, confident
- Specific call to action
- Reference their timeline if mentioned

**Good:**
> "I'd love to discuss how my experience with complex B2B tools could help Linear's design team. I'm available this week if you'd like to chat."

**Bad:**
> "Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to the opportunity to discuss how I can contribute to your team's success. Please feel free to reach out at your earliest convenience."

---

## Length Guidelines

- **Total:** 250-400 words
- **Each paragraph:** 2-4 sentences
- **Err short** — hiring managers skim

---

## What to Include

- [ ] Specific company/role reference (not "this position")
- [ ] One portfolio project with metric
- [ ] Connection between your work and their needs
- [ ] Clear call to action

---

## What to Avoid

- Generic opening ("I am writing to apply...")
- Restating your resume ("I have X years of experience...")
- Listing skills without context
- Closing with "please feel free to reach out"
- Any phrase from the "bad examples" above

---

## Checklist Before Sending

- [ ] Did I mention the company name specifically?
- [ ] Did I reference a specific project with a result?
- [ ] Does it fit on one page?
- [ ] Did I remove all generic phrases?
- [ ] Would I be embarrassed if 1000 other applicants sent the same thing?
