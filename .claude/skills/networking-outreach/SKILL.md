# Networking Outreach

Generate genuine networking messages to connect with designers, build relationships, and expand your professional network.

## Usage

```
/networking-outreach
```

Then provide details about who you want to connect with.

## What It Does

1. Generates personalized outreach messages for different networking scenarios
2. Provides follow-up templates to maintain relationships
3. Guides you on building genuine connections (not transactional networking)

## Why This Matters

- **Your network determines your opportunities** — most jobs come through connections
- **Designers undernetwork** — we focus on craft, not relationships
- **Genuine connections > large networks** — 20 real relationships beat 2000 LinkedIn connections
- **Network before you need it** — building relationships during job search is too late

## Instructions for Claude

When the user runs `/networking-outreach`:

### Phase 1: Context Gathering

1. Read context files:
   - `context/resume.md` for background
   - `context/portfolio.md` for work to reference
   - `context/writing-style.md` for tone

2. Ask for:
   - Who they want to connect with (name, role, company)
   - How they found them (LinkedIn, Twitter, conference, mutual connection, etc.)
   - Why they want to connect (genuine interest, not "to get a job")
   - Any specific common ground (same school, similar projects, shared interests)

### Phase 2: Determine Scenario

3. Identify the networking scenario:
   - **Cold outreach**: No prior connection
   - **Warm outreach**: Mutual connection or shared experience
   - **Follow-up from event**: Met at conference, meetup, etc.
   - **Reconnecting**: Haven't talked in a while
   - **Giving value**: Sharing something helpful
   - **Congratulating**: New role, launch, achievement

### Phase 3: Generate Message

4. Create outreach message based on scenario:

**Core Principles (All Scenarios):**
- **Lead with them, not you** — reference their work, not your needs
- **Be specific** — generic messages get ignored
- **Give before you ask** — offer value, insight, or genuine appreciation
- **Keep it short** — 3-5 sentences max
- **No hidden agenda** — if you want something, be upfront

### Scenario Templates

**Cold Outreach (Admire Their Work)**
```
Hi [Name] — I came across your [specific work: case study, talk, article, project] and wanted to reach out.

[Specific thing you appreciated and WHY it resonated]. I'm working on [related thing] and your approach gave me a new perspective.

Just wanted to say thanks for sharing your work.

[Your name]
```

**Cold Outreach (Similar Background)**
```
Hi [Name] — I noticed we both [shared experience: same school, same previous company, similar career path].

I'm currently [your situation] and saw your work at [Company]. Your [specific project or approach] caught my attention.

Would love to connect — always great to know fellow [shared background] designers.

[Your name]
```

**Warm Outreach (Mutual Connection)**
```
Hi [Name] — [Mutual connection] suggested I reach out. We were talking about [topic], and they mentioned your perspective on [specific thing].

I'm a [your role] working on [relevant context], and I'd love to hear your take sometime.

[Your name]
```

**Follow-Up from Event**
```
Hi [Name] — We met briefly at [event] after your [talk/session/at the booth]. Your point about [specific thing] stuck with me.

I've been thinking about it since — especially how it applies to [your context]. Would love to continue the conversation sometime.

[Your name]
```

**Reconnecting (Haven't Talked in a While)**
```
Hi [Name] — It's been a while since [context: we worked together, met at X, connected]. Hope you're doing well.

I saw [recent thing about them: new role, project, post] — congrats! I've been [brief update on you].

Would love to catch up if you have time.

[Your name]
```

**Giving Value (Sharing Something Helpful)**
```
Hi [Name] — Saw your recent [post/project/question] about [topic] and thought of this [resource/article/tool].

[Brief context on why it's relevant to them].

Hope it's helpful!

[Your name]
```

**Congratulating**
```
Hi [Name] — Congrats on [achievement: new role, launch, award, milestone]!

[Specific thing you appreciate about it]. Really well-deserved.

[Your name]
```

### Phase 4: Follow-Up Guidance

5. Provide follow-up cadence:

**If they respond:**
- Continue the conversation naturally
- Don't immediately ask for anything
- Look for ways to be helpful

**If they don't respond:**
- Wait 1-2 weeks, then try ONE more time
- Reference something new (their recent post, relevant article, etc.)
- If still no response, let it go — they may connect later

**Maintaining relationships:**
- Check in every 4-8 weeks
- Share relevant articles or resources
- Congratulate them on wins
- Engage with their content
- Offer introductions when you can

### Phase 5: Output

6. Output:
   - Personalized outreach message
   - Follow-up template (if no response)
   - Ongoing connection ideas

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't pitch immediately** — relationships first, asks later
- **Don't be generic** — "I'd love to connect and learn from you" is forgettable
- **Don't connect and disappear** — if you reach out, follow through
- **Don't only network when job hunting** — build relationships before you need them
- **Don't keep score** — genuine generosity comes back around
- **Don't mass message** — quality over quantity always

## The "Give First" Mindset

Best networkers give more than they take:

**Ways to give value:**
- Share a relevant article or resource
- Make an introduction they'd benefit from
- Offer feedback on their work (if appropriate)
- Engage thoughtfully with their content
- Share job opportunities you're not pursuing
- Amplify their work to your network

**The return:**
- People remember who helped them
- Givers build stronger networks
- When you need help, people want to help you back

## Building a Networking Habit

**Weekly:**
- Engage with 3-5 people's content meaningfully
- Send 1-2 genuine outreach messages
- Respond to anyone who reached out to you

**Monthly:**
- Catch up with 2-3 existing connections
- Attend one design event (virtual or in-person)
- Share something valuable (article, insight, resource)

**Quarterly:**
- Review your network — who haven't you talked to?
- Make strategic introductions
- Reflect: who's helped you? How can you reciprocate?

## Notes

- **Genuine curiosity beats networking tactics** — be interested, not interesting
- **Consistency > intensity** — small touches over time build relationships
- **Online + offline** — best networks combine both
- **Your network is an asset** — invest in it like your portfolio
- **Play the long game** — some connections take years to develop
