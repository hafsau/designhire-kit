# Informational Interview

Reach out to designers at target companies for informational conversations that build relationships and can lead to referrals.

## Usage

```
/informational-interview
```

Then provide the person's name, role, and company.

## What It Does

1. Generates a personalized outreach message
2. Provides conversation structure and questions to ask
3. Creates follow-up templates
4. Guides you on turning conversations into referrals (without being pushy)

## Why This Matters

- **85% of jobs** are filled through networking
- **70% of jobs** are never publicly advertised
- Informational interviews are **low-pressure relationship building** — not asking for a job
- Designers who network get roles faster and negotiate better

## Instructions for Claude

When the user runs `/informational-interview`:

### Phase 1: Context Gathering

1. Read context files:
   - `context/resume.md` for background
   - `context/portfolio.md` for relevant projects
   - `context/writing-style.md` for tone

2. Ask for:
   - Person's name and current role
   - Company they work at
   - How you found them (LinkedIn, mutual connection, conference, etc.)
   - What you want to learn (company culture, their career path, specific role, etc.)
   - Any mutual connections or shared experiences

### Phase 2: Research

3. Research the person (if web search available):
   - Their career path and background
   - Recent posts, talks, or articles they've shared
   - Projects they've worked on
   - Mutual connections or shared experiences

### Phase 3: Generate Outreach

4. Create outreach message following these rules:

**Structure:**
- **Hook**: Reference something specific about them (not generic praise)
- **Connection**: Why you're reaching out to THEM specifically
- **Ask**: Clear, low-commitment request (15-20 min chat)
- **Easy out**: Make it easy to say no

**Tone:**
- Genuine curiosity, not transactional
- Respectful of their time
- Specific about what you want to learn
- NOT asking for a job (that comes later, organically)

**Length:** 4-6 sentences max. Busy people skim.

### Phase 4: Conversation Guide

5. Provide conversation structure:

```
## Before the Call
- Review their LinkedIn, portfolio, recent work
- Prepare 5-7 questions (you won't ask all)
- Have your "tell me about yourself" ready (60 seconds max)

## Conversation Flow (20-30 min)

Opening (2 min):
- Thank them for their time
- Brief intro of yourself (who you are, what you're exploring)

Their Story (10 min):
- "How did you end up at [Company]?"
- "What's your day-to-day like?"
- "What do you enjoy most about working there?"

The Role/Company (10 min):
- "What does the design team structure look like?"
- "What's the biggest design challenge you're working on?"
- "What surprised you about working there?"

Wrap-Up (5 min):
- "What advice would you give someone trying to break into [X]?"
- "Is there anyone else you'd recommend I talk to?"
- Thank them genuinely

## Questions to Ask

**About Their Path:**
- "What led you to [Company]?"
- "What's something you wish you knew before joining?"

**About the Team:**
- "How is the design team structured?"
- "How do designers collaborate with PMs and engineers?"
- "What's the design culture like?"

**About the Work:**
- "What projects are you most excited about?"
- "What's a design challenge you're working through?"

**Forward-Looking:**
- "What skills are becoming more important for designers there?"
- "Is there anyone else you'd suggest I connect with?"

## Questions to AVOID
- "Are you hiring?" (too direct, too early)
- "Can you refer me?" (let them offer)
- "What's the salary like?" (inappropriate for info interview)
- Anything you could Google yourself
```

### Phase 5: Follow-Up Templates

6. Provide follow-up templates:

**Same-Day Thank You (within 24 hours):**
```
Subject: Thanks for chatting today

Hi [Name],

Thanks for taking the time to chat. Your insight about [specific thing they said] was really helpful — it's making me rethink [how you'll apply it].

I'll definitely [action you mentioned: check out that resource, connect with that person, etc.].

Really appreciate it.

[Your name]
```

**If They Mentioned Someone to Connect With:**
```
Subject: [Name] suggested I reach out

Hi [New Person],

[Mutual Contact] suggested I reach out. I'm a [your role] exploring [what you're exploring], and they mentioned you'd have great perspective on [specific topic].

Would you have 15-20 minutes for a quick chat? Happy to work around your schedule.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

**2-4 Weeks Later (Stay in Touch):**
```
Subject: Update + that article you mentioned

Hi [Name],

Hope you're doing well. I wanted to share an update — since we talked, I [action you took based on their advice].

Also, I came across [article/resource related to something they mentioned] and thought of our conversation.

Thanks again for the guidance.

[Your name]
```

**When a Role Opens Up (If Relationship is Warm):**
```
Subject: [Role] at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I saw [Company] posted a [Role] position. Based on our conversation, it seems like exactly what I've been looking for.

I'd love to apply. If you think I'd be a good fit, would you be open to referring me? No pressure either way — I really value our connection regardless.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

### Phase 6: Output

7. Output:
   - Personalized outreach message
   - 5-7 tailored questions based on what they want to learn
   - Thank-you template
   - Guidance on next steps

## Example Outreach Messages

**Example 1: Found on LinkedIn**
```
Hi Sarah — I came across your case study on the Linear mobile redesign and loved how you approached the keyboard navigation problem.

I'm a product designer exploring opportunities in developer tools, and Linear's design approach is exactly what I'm interested in. I'd love to hear about your experience there — especially how the team handles complex interaction design.

Would you have 15-20 minutes for a quick chat? Totally understand if you're swamped.

[Name]
```

**Example 2: Mutual Connection**
```
Hi Marcus — [Mutual friend] suggested I reach out. I'm a designer transitioning from healthcare to fintech, and she mentioned you made a similar switch a few years ago.

I'd love to hear how you navigated that transition — what skills translated, what you had to learn, any surprises.

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call?

[Name]
```

**Example 3: After Conference/Event**
```
Hi Jamie — We briefly met at Config last week after your talk on design systems. Your point about token naming conventions stuck with me.

I'm building a design system at my current company and running into similar challenges. Would you have time for a quick chat about how you approached governance?

[Name]
```

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't ask for a job** — this is relationship building, not a job application
- **Don't be generic** — "I'd love to learn about your career" is forgettable
- **Don't ask for too much time** — 15-20 minutes is the sweet spot
- **Don't skip the follow-up** — the thank-you is where relationships are built
- **Don't ghost if they say no** — thank them anyway, they may help later
- **Don't connect then immediately pitch** — build rapport first

## Converting to Referrals (Organically)

The goal isn't to ask for referrals. It's to build relationships where referrals happen naturally.

**Signs they might refer you:**
- They offer to introduce you to others
- They ask what kind of roles you're looking for
- They mention upcoming openings
- They say "let me know how I can help"

**If they offer to help:**
- Be specific: "A referral to the [Role] would be amazing"
- Make it easy: Send your resume and the job link
- Thank them regardless of outcome

**If they don't offer:**
- That's okay — not every conversation leads to a referral
- Stay in touch every 4-6 weeks
- When a role opens, you can reach out warmly

## Notes

- **Quality over quantity** — 5 genuine conversations beat 50 cold messages
- **Give before you ask** — share articles, make introductions, be helpful
- **Play the long game** — some relationships take months to develop
- **Be genuinely curious** — people can tell when you're transactional
- **Follow up** — most people don't, so you'll stand out
